About the Journal

The Study Journal is dedicated to helping students of all ages improve their learning techniques using scientifically proven methods. We share insights from research in neuroscience, psychology, and cognitive science to help you optimize your study time and achieve better results.

Top Study Tips

  • 1. Focused vs. Diffuse Thinking

    Alternate between focused attention and relaxed, diffuse thinking. This helps you form connections between ideas and enhance your problem-solving abilities.

  • 2. Spaced Repetition

    Review material over increasingly spaced intervals. This technique, known as spaced repetition, has been shown to improve long-term retention of information.

  • 3. Practice Retrieval

    Testing yourself on material, rather than just re-reading it, strengthens memory and understanding. Use quizzes and flashcards to practice retrieval.

  • 4. Chunking Information

    Group related pieces of information together. Chunking helps you process complex information more easily by organizing it into meaningful categories.
